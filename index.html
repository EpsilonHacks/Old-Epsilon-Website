Solving Tomorrow's Problems Today

Date: March 14-15
Location: Make School, San Francisco

About

EpsilonHacks is a hackathon run by students from the Fremont Unified School District who aim to give students the chance to experience an essential culture element of the Bay Area. Our mission is to provide students the opportunity to technologically innovate solutions for socio-economic issues within our communities. We want to challenge each and every student to create something extraordinary, to truly shape the community they reside in, and be rewarded for the change they create.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend?

Any high school student can attend! Absolutely no coding experience is necessary!

How do I form or join a team?

There will be a team mixer at the beginning of the hackathon for you to meet others and form teams. We encourage you to come and meet new people! Each team may have a maximum of four people.

Does this cost money?

No! Epsilon Hacks is completely free for all participants â€” food, swag, and other costs are covered.

What should I bring?

Please bring a water bottle, laptop, chargers, and the signed waiver. Sleeping bag and toileteries are highly recommended as it's overnight.

What if I have dietary restrictions?

No problem! If you have dietary restrictions, simply add them to your application when you register, and we will accommodate you!

What if I can't code?

No problem! Experience isn't required to attend. Moreover, we will have various workshops where you can learn!

What if I have another question?

Feel free to reach out to us at epsilonhacks@gmail.com! We will get back to you ASAP!
